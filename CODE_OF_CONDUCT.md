# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, maintainers, sponsors, and leaders of Plantaah pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Respecting project maintainers, contributors, and users.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment or intimidation.
- Public or private humiliation.
- Posting inappropriate or offensive content.
- Personal attacks.
- Doxxing or publishing private information without consent.
- Disruptive, abusive, or threatening conduct.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Plantaah community spaces, including repository discussions, pull requests, issues, chat channels, mailing lists, project events, sponsor communications related to the project, and any other space where the community communicates.

It applies whether participation is voluntary, sponsored, or part of paid maintainership or governance work.

## Enforcement

Violations may be reported privately to the Planta project team at the designated contact address.

The project team will review and respond to reports as promptly as practical. All reports will be handled with respect for confidentiality and fairness.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may take any of the following actions in response to violations:

- A private warning.
- A public warning.
- A temporary ban from community participation.
- A permanent ban from community participation.

In serious cases, the project team may remove access to project communication channels immediately.

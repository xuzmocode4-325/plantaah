# Plantaah Collaborator Agreement

**Effective Date:** [Insert date]  
**Project:** Plantaah

This Collaborator Agreement governs the role of approved collaborators who participate in the maintenance, research, development, and review of the Plantaah repository and related project operations.

This agreement is intended for collaborators who function as part of the repo maintenance team, actively engage in research and development, and review and accept or reject public contributions.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this agreement is to define the responsibilities, authority, confidentiality obligations, and operating rules for Plantaah collaborators so that the project can maintain quality, security, and mission alignment while supporting an open-source community.

## 2. Parties

This agreement applies to each person or organization that has been accepted as a collaborator by the Plantaah maintainers or project stewards.

A collaborator may be:
- A maintainer.
- A reviewer.
- A research and development contributor.
- A release contributor.
- A governance participant, if separately designated.

## 3. Role of Collaborators

Collaborators are expected to contribute to one or more of the following:

- Maintaining the repository.
- Reviewing public contributions.
- Accepting or rejecting pull requests.
- Researching and improving product functionality.
- Improving documentation, testing, and release quality.
- Supporting roadmap planning and technical decision-making.
- Assisting with security, reliability, and operational readiness.

Collaborators are part of the project’s stewardship function and are expected to act in the best interests of Plantaah’s open-source mission.

## 4. Authority

Unless otherwise limited in writing, collaborators may:

- Review pull requests.
- Comment on issues and proposals.
- Recommend changes or rejection of contributions.
- Merge approved changes if they have merge authority.
- Participate in release readiness discussions.
- Participate in technical roadmap discussions.

No collaborator may represent themselves as having authority beyond the scope granted to them by Plantaah.

## 5. Contribution Review Standards

Collaborators who review public contributions must evaluate changes based on:

- Technical correctness.
- Security.
- Documentation quality.
- Alignment with the project roadmap.
- Compatibility with existing architecture.
- Legal or privacy risk.
- Maintenance burden.

Collaborators should reject or request changes to contributions that materially harm code quality, security, or the project mission.

## 6. Responsibilities

Each collaborator agrees to:

- Act in good faith.
- Review contributions in a timely manner.
- Communicate respectfully and professionally.
- Follow the Code of Conduct.
- Preserve the confidentiality of non-public project information.
- Disclose conflicts of interest that may affect decision-making.
- Avoid using collaborator privileges for personal gain or favoritism.

## 7. Intellectual Property

All contributions made to the Plantaah repository are subject to the project’s applicable license and contribution policy.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing:
- Contributors retain ownership of their pre-existing intellectual property.
- Contributions to the repository are licensed under the repository’s open-source license.
- This agreement does not transfer ownership of the open-source codebase unless a separate written assignment or contributor license agreement says otherwise.

Collaborators must not introduce code, data, or materials that they do not have the right to contribute.

## 8. Confidentiality

During collaboration, a collaborator may receive access to confidential information, including:
- Private roadmap discussions.
- Security issues.
- Unreleased features.
- Partner or sponsor communications.
- Internal operational details.
- Business planning materials.
- Contributor identity or contact data, where protected.

Collaborators must not disclose confidential information outside the project unless authorized or required by law.

This obligation continues even after the collaborator stops participating.

## 9. Security and Vulnerabilities

If a collaborator discovers a vulnerability, they must follow the project’s security reporting process and handle the information discreetly.

Collaborators may not publicly disclose security issues before coordinated release or approval from the project’s security process, except where required by law or immediate safety concerns.

## 10. Non-Commercial and Commercial Separation

Plantaah’s open-source repo, nonprofit mission, and commercial service layer are related but distinct.

Collaborators agree that:
- The open-source repository is maintained for the public mission.
- Premium subscription products, analytics tools, hosted dashboards, and commercial notifications are separate commercial offerings.
- Repo collaboration does not grant ownership of the commercial service.
- Collaborators may not promise commercial features, pricing, or guarantees unless authorized to do so.

## 11. Decision-Making

Collaborators may participate in technical decisions, but final authority rests with the project’s documented governance structure.

Where consensus cannot be reached, the designated maintainers or stewards may decide based on the project’s governance rules.

## 12. Time Commitment and Activity

A collaborator is expected to remain reasonably active in the areas they are assigned to support.

If a collaborator becomes inactive for a prolonged period, the project may reduce or revoke their review or merge authority, while allowing them to remain a community participant.

## 13. Compensation

At present, collaborator participation may be voluntary unless a separate written agreement states otherwise.

If Plantaah later pays collaborators, any salary, stipend, grant, or contractor arrangement must be documented separately and will not change the terms of this agreement unless expressly stated.

## 14. Conflicts of Interest

Collaborators must disclose any conflict of interest that could affect their judgment, including:
- Financial interests.
- Employer interests.
- Sponsor relationships.
- Competing product interests.
- Personal relationships that could affect fairness.

A collaborator with a conflict may be asked to recuse themselves from a decision.

## 15. Termination

Plantaah may revoke collaborator status if a collaborator:
- Violates this agreement.
- Violates the Code of Conduct.
- Misuses review authority.
- Breaches confidentiality.
- Acts against the project’s mission or governance rules.
- Becomes inactive for an extended time.

A collaborator may resign at any time by notifying the project maintainers.

## 16. No Partnership or Employment

Unless separately documented, this agreement does not create:
- A legal partnership.
- An employment relationship.
- A joint venture.
- Agency authority.
- Profit-sharing rights.

## 17. Governing Documents

This agreement should be read together with:
- `CONTRIBUTING.md`
- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`
- `GOVERNANCE.md`
- `SECURITY.md`
- `LICENSE`
- `COMMERCIAL_TERMS.md` if applicable

If there is any conflict, the governing project documents and any separately signed legal agreement will control.

## 18. Changes

Plantaah may update this agreement from time to time. Continued participation after notice of an updated version indicates acceptance of the revised terms.

## 19. Contact

Questions about collaborator status or this agreement should be directed to the Plantaah project maintainers or the designated project steward.

# Plantaah Funding

Plantaah is supported through a mix of donations, sponsorships, grants, and commercial service revenue.

This document explains the funding paths for the open-source project and how those paths relate to the nonprofit core and the commercial hosted service layer.

## 1. Funding Sources

Plantaah may receive support from:

- Individual donations.
- Organizational sponsorships.
- Grants and fiscal support.
- In-kind contributions such as cloud credits, hosting, tools, or services.
- Paid commercial usage of the hosted API or web application.
- Subscriptions for managed or premium service features.

## 2. Open Source vs Commercial Funding

Funding for the open-source project supports the public mission, including:

- Core development.
- Maintenance.
- Security work.
- Documentation.
- Community operations.
- Governance and administration.

Commercial revenue supports the hosted service layer, operations, support, and related business functions.

Open-source donations and sponsorships do not automatically purchase commercial access, premium support, or special product rights unless explicitly stated in writing.

## 4. Use of Funds

Funds may be used for:

- Infrastructure and hosting.
- Development and maintenance.
- Security and vulnerability response.
- Documentation and release work.
- Community support and moderation.
- Compensation for maintainers, contributors, or project staff where approved.
- Administrative and legal expenses related to the project.

## 5. Transparency and Governance

Plantaah aims to manage funding transparently and in line with project governance.

Material funding decisions should be made according to the project's governance process and any applicable nonprofit or stewardship rules.

## 6. No Control Rights

Funding does not give donors or sponsors control over the project unless a separate written agreement says otherwise.

Specifically, funding does not by itself grant:

- Ownership of the codebase.
- Trademark rights.
- Roadmap control.
- Access to private development channels.
- Special treatment in technical decisions.

## 7. Conditional or Restricted Funding

If a sponsor or donor wants their funding used for a specific purpose, that restriction must be accepted by the project in writing.

Plantaah may decline restricted funding that conflicts with the project mission or governance model.

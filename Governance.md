# Plantaah Governance

## 1. Purpose

This document describes how Plantaah is governed, how decisions are made, who maintains the project, and how the open-source project relates to the nonprofit and commercial services built on top of it.

Plantaah is an open-source project with a public codebase, a nonprofit-oriented core mission, and a commercial hosted offering based on the same underlying software.

## 2. Project Structure

Plantaah operates across three related but distinct layers:

- **Open-source core**: the public repository, licensed for community use, cloning, modification, and contribution.
- **Nonprofit core**: the mission-driven entity or operating body that may receive donations, sponsorships, and grants to support the project.
- **Commercial services**: hosted API access, subscription-based services, and proprietary user interfaces built on top of the open-source core.

These layers are related in mission but separate in function. Contributions to the open-source repository do not grant rights to proprietary branding, hosted infrastructure, or commercial service terms unless explicitly stated in writing.

## 3. Governance Principles

Plantaah is governed according to the following principles:

- Transparency in project direction and major decisions.
- Merit-based contribution and review.
- Respect for the open-source community.
- Protection of the nonprofit mission from undue commercial influence.
- Clear separation between public repository governance and commercial service operations.
- Security, privacy, and responsible stewardship of user data.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing and merging pull requests.
- Triaging issues.
- Managing releases.
- Enforcing contribution standards.
- Coordinating roadmap priorities.
- Escalating security-sensitive concerns to the security process.

### 4.2 Reviewers

Reviewers may comment on pull requests, suggest changes, and participate in technical evaluation, but they do not have merge authority unless also appointed as maintainers.

### 4.3 Release Managers

Release managers coordinate version tagging, changelogs, and release readiness. They may be appointed from the maintainer group or designated separately.

### 4.4 Project Stewards

Project stewards are responsible for the long-term mission of Plantaah and may represent the nonprofit or community governance layer. They ensure that the open-source project remains aligned with its public purpose.

### 4.5 Nonprofit Board or Equivalent Body

If Plantaah is operated through a nonprofit entity, the board or equivalent governing body is responsible for:

- Financial oversight.
- Donation and sponsorship policy.
- Approval of paid maintainer roles.
- Conflicts of interest.
- Any inter-entity agreement with a commercial operator.

## 5. Decision-Making

### 5.1 Routine Decisions

Routine technical decisions may be made by maintainers through normal pull request review and issue discussion.

### 5.2 Significant Decisions

Significant decisions include:

- Changes to project governance.
- Adoption or change of license.
- Major roadmap shifts.
- Introduction of a CLA or DCO policy.
- Creation of a commercial spinout or new service boundary.
- Changes affecting nonprofit funds, sponsorships, or maintainer compensation.

Significant decisions should be discussed publicly where appropriate and approved by the maintainer group and, where relevant, the nonprofit stewards or board.

### 5.3 Consensus and Voting

The project should first seek consensus. If consensus cannot be reached, maintainers may vote.

Unless otherwise stated, a simple majority of maintainers is sufficient for technical decisions. Higher thresholds may be required for governance, licensing, or structural changes.

## 6. Maintainer Appointment

New maintainers may be appointed based on sustained contribution, technical judgment, and alignment with the project’s mission.

A maintainer candidate should typically:

- Have a history of high-quality contributions.
- Demonstrate good communication and judgment.
- Understand the open-source and nonprofit mission of Plantaah.
- Be accepted by existing maintainers through a documented process.

## 7. Maintainer Removal

A maintainer may be removed for:

- Voluntary resignation.
- Prolonged inactivity.
- Breach of the Code of Conduct.
- Misuse of authority.
- Actions that materially damage the project.

Removal should be documented and, where possible, handled with respect and due process.

## 8. Community Participation

Community contributors are encouraged to participate through issues, pull requests, documentation, testing, design input, and discussions.

Community members may influence the project through:

- Public discussion.
- Code contributions.
- Documentation and testing.
- Feedback on roadmap proposals.
- Participation in community surveys or consultations.

## 9. Nonprofit and Commercial Separation

Plantaah’s open-source governance must remain distinct from any commercial business using the same codebase.

The following boundaries should remain clear:

- The nonprofit exists to support the mission and community of Plantaah.
- The commercial offering may provide hosted API access, subscriptions, or proprietary UX.
- Open-source contributors do not automatically acquire rights to commercial infrastructure or revenue.
- Commercial priorities must not override the public mission without documented governance review.

If an inter-entity agreement exists between the nonprofit and a commercial operator, that agreement should define IP use, trademark use, staffing, service terms, and revenue boundaries.

## 10. Financial Stewardship

If Plantaah receives donations, sponsorships, or grants, those funds should be used in line with the project mission and any donor restrictions.

Funds may be used for:

- Infrastructure.
- Maintenance.
- Security work.
- Documentation.
- Community support.
- Paid maintainer or governance roles where approved.

Any payment of salaries or contractor fees should be governed by a written policy and approved through the nonprofit or stewardship body.

## 11. Records and Transparency

The project should maintain reasonable public records of:

- Maintainer roles.
- Major governance decisions.
- Release decisions.
- Policies affecting contributions or security.
- Nonprofit or stewardship arrangements, where public disclosure is appropriate.

## 12. Changes to This Document

This document may be updated by the maintainers and, where necessary, by the nonprofit stewards or board. Significant changes should be announced publicly.

## 13. Contact

Governance questions should be directed to the Plantaah maintainers or the official project contact address listed in the repository.

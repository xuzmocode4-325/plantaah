# Plantaah Sponsorship and Donations Policy

**Effective Date:** [Insert date]  
**Project Name:** Plantaah

This document explains how Plantaah accepts donations and sponsorships, how those funds are used, and what donors and sponsors may reasonably expect in return.

Plantaah is an open-source project with a public repository, a nonprofit-supported core mission, and separate commercial services. Donations and sponsorships support the open-source project, community operations, infrastructure, maintenance, governance, documentation, and related mission work.

## 1. Purpose of Donations and Sponsorships

Plantaah welcomes donations and sponsorships from individuals, organizations, and institutions that support the project’s mission.

Contributions may be used for:

- Hosting and infrastructure.
- Development and maintenance.
- Security work.
- Documentation and community support.
- Governance and administrative overhead.
- Compensation for active maintainers, stewards, or contractors, if approved.
- Other mission-aligned project costs.

## 2. No Ownership or Control

A donation or sponsorship does not give the donor or sponsor ownership of Plantaah.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing:

- Donors do not receive control over the roadmap.
- Sponsors do not gain voting rights.
- Sponsors do not acquire rights to the codebase beyond the applicable open-source license.
- Sponsors do not receive rights to the Plantaah name, logo, or branding.

## 3. Sponsorship vs Donation

A **donation** is a voluntary contribution made to support Plantaah’s mission.

A **sponsorship** is a contribution made in exchange for recognition or agreed promotional benefits, if offered by the project.

Plantaah may distinguish between:
- General donations.
- Sponsored funding for infrastructure or maintainers.
- Restricted gifts for a specific purpose.
- In-kind sponsorships, such as hosting credits, tools, or services.

## 4. Use of Funds

Funds will be used in a manner consistent with Plantaah’s mission and any restrictions attached to a contribution.

If a donor specifies a restricted purpose, Plantaah will use the funds for that purpose where feasible and lawful. If the restricted purpose cannot be honored, Plantaah may decline the gift or request written approval for an alternative use.

## 5. Transparency

Plantaah aims to maintain reasonable transparency around:
- Received donations and sponsorships.
- Funding categories.
- Project-supported roles.
- Material sponsorship arrangements.

Where appropriate, Plantaah may publish sponsor acknowledgments, funding summaries, or annual reports.

## 6. Sponsor Recognition

Plantaah may offer sponsor recognition such as:

- Listing on the website or repository.
- Name or logo placement on a sponsor page.
- Public thanks in release notes or community updates.
- Other recognition agreed in writing.

Recognition does not imply endorsement, partnership, or control unless explicitly stated.

## 7. Sponsor Independence

Sponsors remain independent from Plantaah.

Unless a separate written agreement says otherwise:
- Sponsors may not direct technical decisions.
- Sponsors may not require inclusion of proprietary features in the open-source core.
- Sponsors may not override contributor governance.
- Sponsors may not require exclusive access to the roadmap or source code.

## 8. Compensation of Maintainers

Plantaah may use donated or sponsored funds to pay maintainers, governance roles, contractors, or support personnel when approved through the project’s governance process.

Paid roles do not create special ownership of the project unless separately agreed.

## 9. Tax and Legal Treatment

The tax treatment of donations or sponsorships depends on the donor’s location, the operating entity receiving the funds, and applicable law.

Plantaah does not provide tax advice. Donors and sponsors should consult their own advisors where needed.

## 10. Payment Processing

Donations or sponsorships may be processed through third-party payment providers.

Processing fees, chargebacks, refunds, and payment terms may vary depending on the provider and the selected payment method.

## 11. Refunds

Donations are generally non-refundable unless required by law or expressly stated otherwise.

Sponsorship refunds, if any, will be governed by the applicable sponsorship agreement or written terms.

## 12. Restrictions and Compliance

Plantaah may decline or return donations or sponsorships that:
- Conflict with the project’s mission.
- Create legal, reputational, or operational risk.
- Require control or obligations inconsistent with open-source governance.
- Would jeopardize nonprofit status or project independence, if applicable.

## 13. Relationship to Commercial Services

Donations and sponsorships support the open-source project and mission work.

They do not purchase hosted API access, premium support, subscription features, or other commercial services unless those services are separately purchased under the commercial terms.

## 14. Changes to This Policy

Plantaah may update this policy from time to time. The current version will be published in the repository or on the official project site.

## 15. Contact

For sponsorship or donation inquiries, contact the Plantaah project through the official project email or stewardship contact listed in the repository.

## 3. Permitted Uses

You may use the Plantaah name in a descriptive, truthful way to refer to the project or its software, provided that your use does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or official affiliation.

- “This tool integrates with Plantaah.”
- “A guide for deploying the Plantaah open-source project.”
- “Based on Plantaah open-source technology.”

You may also use the name in factual discussions, reviews, academic work, tutorials, news reporting, and community commentary, so long as the use is accurate and non-misleading.

You must obtain written permission before using the Plantaah marks in:

## 5. Prohibited Uses

You may not:

- Use Plantaah marks in a way that suggests your project is official, approved, sponsored, or maintained by Plantaah unless that is true and authorized.

- Present a fork, derivative work, or hosted service as if it were the official Plantaah service unless expressly authorized.

If you create a fork or derivative version of Plantaah, you should use your own distinct name and branding.

For modified versions, the safest practice is to remove the Plantaah logo and distinctive brand identifiers and replace them with your own.

Descriptive phrases such as “hosting for Plantaah” or “Plantaah integration services” may be acceptable if they are truthful, accurate, and clearly identify the provider as independent.
